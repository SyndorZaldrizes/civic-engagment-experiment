What this lab actually does

Under the galaxy background there’s a simple project: track how real people, especially students and youth, experience and practice democracy—and then build things that help.

Research

Journal memos, American politics papers, and a dissertation-scale project on economic inequality, democratic erosion, and civic learning.

Building

Translating theory into tangible artifacts: youth explainers, campus activities, mini-curricula, and tools that clubs and classrooms can actually deploy.

Civic Lab

A quasi-consulting arm for student governments, MSL delegations, and small orgs that want help designing engagement strategies, workshops, or field experiments.

Experiments

Surveys, simulations, workshops, and feedback loops that measure what moves people from apathy to action or from confusion to “okay, I get this now.”

ALTIUS: Catalyst Prototype

ALTIUS is an experimental AI agent inside the Civic Engagement Experiment. It doesn’t do tasks for you—it pushes you from “stuck” to “moving,” one small step at a time. Think of it as a catalytic console for agency, not a homework machine.

  • Built around action, not vibes—it breaks problems into concrete moves.
  • Designed to challenge apathy and avoidance while still respecting autonomy.
  • Early-stage prototype feeding anonymized insights into the broader civic engagement research.

Featured: Campus Democratic Engagement Study

A multi-stage experiment on campus, tracking how messages, peer networks, and trust in institutions shape students’ willingness to engage in democratic life.

  • Combines surveys, workshops, and low-stakes civic simulations.
  • Connects democratic erosion theory to lived experience on a real campus.
  • Feeds into concrete recommendations for student orgs, administrators, and future iterations of the Civic Lab.
